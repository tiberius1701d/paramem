Review extracted personal facts from a voice assistant conversation. The facts and transcript are anonymized — entities appear as bare placeholders (Person_1, City_1, Thing_1). KEEP, MODIFY (resolve coreference / split compound / canonicalize symmetric / dedupe synonyms), or ADD facts directly stated by the transcript. Do NOT drop facts — a separate plausibility filter handles removal.


## Brace-binding contract — REQUIRED for every NEW entity you mint

You may introduce new placeholders for entities the extractor missed (an event, topic, location, project, etc. named in the transcript). Pre-existing anonymizer placeholders stay BARE (`Person_1`, `City_1`); placeholders YOU mint must use BRACED form (`{{Event_1}}`).

Every new braced placeholder MUST appear in BOTH:
- `updated_transcript` — the placeholder substituted at the exact position of the real-text span it replaces.
- `new_entity_bindings` — a dict entry `"Prefix_N": "<exact transcript span>"` (key WITHOUT braces).

Type-appropriate prefixes: Person, City, Country, Org, Event, Topic, Location, Thing. Allocate the smallest free index above all observed values for that prefix (count BOTH bare and braced occurrences).

POSITIVE — transcript: `"...Person_1 led the agile transformation initiative at Org_1..."`
→ facts: `led(Person_1, {{Event_1}})`, `works_at(Person_1, Org_1)`
→ updated_transcript: `"...Person_1 led {{Event_1}} at Org_1..."`
→ new_entity_bindings: `{{"Event_1": "the agile transformation initiative"}}`

POSITIVE (role-instance — bind co-temporal attributes to ONE entity, not flat triples on the speaker) — transcript: `"...Person_1 was Senior Engineer at Org_1 in City_1 from Jan 2014 to Sep 2017..."`
→ facts: `held_role(Person_1, {{Role_1}})`, `at_company({{Role_1}}, Org_1)`, `in_location({{Role_1}}, City_1)`, `start_date({{Role_1}}, "Jan 2014")`, `end_date({{Role_1}}, "Sep 2017")`
→ updated_transcript: `"...Person_1 was {{Role_1}} at Org_1 in City_1 from Jan 2014 to Sep 2017..."`
→ new_entity_bindings: `{{"Role_1": "Senior Engineer"}}`

NEGATIVE — same transcript:
- WRONG: `led(Person_1, Project_1)` — bare `Project_1` looks like an anonymizer placeholder but isn't in the mapping → fact dropped at residual sweep.
- WRONG: `led(Person_1, {{Event_1}})` with `Event_1` missing from `new_entity_bindings` → no binding to substitute → fact dropped.
- WRONG: `new_entity_bindings: {{"{{Event_1}}": "..."}}` — key includes braces → lookup misses → fact dropped.
- WRONG (role flattened onto speaker): `held_role(Person_1, "Senior Engineer")`, `worked_at(Person_1, Org_1)`, `lived_in(Person_1, City_1)`, `start_date(Person_1, "Jan 2014")`, `end_date(Person_1, "Sep 2017")` — co-temporal attributes orphan from the role. With multiple roles in one transcript the dates cannot be paired back to a title; downstream reasoning over "what title did Person_1 hold in 2015?" fails.


## Coreference resolution

Few-shot — "my wife is also a teacher"
→ ADD `married_to(Person_1, Person_2)`, `profession(Person_2, "teacher")`.

Few-shot — "we went there last summer" (context: Person_1 + Person_2 + Location_1)
→ ADD `visited(Person_1, Location_1, "last summer")`, `visited(Person_2, Location_1, "last summer")`.

Few-shot — "I" / "me" / "my" → Person_1 (the speaker, unless context indicates otherwise).

Few-shot — "my sister's husband"
→ chain: `sister_of(Person_1, Person_2)`, `married_to(Person_2, Person_3)`.


## Compound fact splitting

Few-shot — "grew up in City_1 and studied in City_2"
→ `grew_up_in(Person_1, City_1)`, `studied_in(Person_1, City_2)`.

Few-shot — "likes hiking and cooking"
→ `likes(Person_1, hiking)`, `likes(Person_1, cooking)`.


## Symmetric predicate canonicalization

Symmetric predicates (one triple represents both directions; lexicographic-min subject wins): friend_of, friends_with, married_to, spouse_of, sibling_of, brother_of, sister_of, cousin_of (when reciprocal), colleague_of, coworker_of, neighbor_of, partner_of, related_to, met_with, knows (only if reciprocal), agrees_with, disagrees_with, attended_with, shares_interest_with.

Few-shot — input has `friend_of(A, B)` AND `friend_of(B, A)`
→ EMIT `friend_of(A, B)` only.

Few-shot — input has `parent_of(A, B)` AND `child_of(B, A)`
→ EMIT BOTH. Asymmetric pair (also: manages/reports_to, taught/studied_under, likes/liked_by) — distinct semantics, keep both directions.


## Predicate de-duplication

Per `(subject, object)` pair, emit AT MOST ONE predicate.

Few-shot — input: `worked_for(P, Org_1)` AND `employed_by(P, Org_1)`
→ EMIT `worked_for(P, Org_1)`. Synonyms; pick the predicate already most frequent in the rest of your output.

Few-shot — input: `parent_of(P, Q)` AND `birth_parent_of(P, Q)`
→ EMIT `birth_parent_of(P, Q)`. Same relation, more specific surface; pick the more specific.


## ADD discipline

Only emit a new fact when the transcript directly states it.

Few-shot — transcript: "scaled team from 15 to 100+ engineers across 4 countries"
→ ADD `scaled_team_from(P, 15 engineers)`, `scaled_team_to(P, 100+ engineers)`, `managed_teams_across(P, 4 countries)`.
→ DO NOT ADD `interviewed/hired/mentored(P, 15 engineers)` — actions not stated.
→ DO NOT broadcast `operates_in(P, Country_X)` for each of the 4 countries — the transcript names a count, not a list.

Few-shot — same artefact named twice ("Indexed Key Retrieval paper", "the parametric memory system")
→ MERGE into one canonical entity; rewrite every relation to that entity. Do not keep two entities with overlapping attributes.


## Output format

Return ONLY a JSON object with exactly three keys:
- `"facts"`: array of `{{subject, predicate, object, relation_type, confidence}}`. New entities appear as `{{Prefix_N}}`.
- `"updated_transcript"`: the anonymized transcript with all new braced placeholders substituted in at their span positions; all other text verbatim.
- `"new_entity_bindings"`: dict mapping each NEW braced placeholder (key WITHOUT braces) to its exact transcript span. Empty `{{}}` if you minted no new entities.

Confidence: 0.9 for coreference-resolved facts, 0.8 for inferred facts, original confidence for unchanged facts.
